Project Overview

American Dream is a story-driven backrooms-styled VR horror game. The protagonist, Peter Locke, is an overworked employee on a business trip staying in a hotel room replicated from the 'New York' hotel in Rotterdam. Through stress-induced hallucinations, he enters 'the backrooms' - a 90's office environment that represents the dull corporate world he's trapped in.

Narrative & Mechanics

Peter receives conflicting voicemails - pressure from his boss threatening termination, and loving messages from his children. In the backrooms, he must evade his menacing boss while collecting his children's drawings from the walls. These posters reveal the children's perspective of their absent, suffering father through symbolic imagery.

Technical Implementation

Originally developed for PC and then ported to VR, this project presented unique challenges in adapting controls and interactions for virtual reality. The team collaborated using Unreal Engine's live editing features, and the game was successfully demonstrated at university presentations.

Project Context

This university project required incorporating three specific elements: the Holland-Amerika plein location, explorer genre gameplay, and VR implementation. We chose Hotel New York from this location, inspiring the "American Dream" title and theme of corporate capitalism's impact on family life.

Holland-Amerika Plein Location

Environment Design

The hotel room was meticulously replicated to provide an authentic starting point before the surreal descent into the backrooms. The children's artwork serves as both collectible gameplay elements and powerful narrative devices showing Peter's impact on his family.

Replicated Hotel Room

Symbolic Elements

The collectible posters reveal deeper themes through children's innocent yet perceptive artwork. They show Peter as absent or suffering, depicting how work stress affects not just him but his entire family's perception of their father.

Symbolic poster created by teammate Nathaniël

Team Collaboration

The development process involved extensive research into collaborative workflows, enabling real-time team editing within Unreal Engine. The final product was successfully demonstrated at university presentations, showcasing both technical achievement and narrative depth.
