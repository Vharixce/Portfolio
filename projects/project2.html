American Dream

Unreal Engine Blueprints Animation Sound Design Widgets Enemy AI Textures Story Line

American Dream is a story driven back-rooms styled VR horror game. The back story is very important in this game. The main character, Peter Locke, is an overworked employee on a business trip. He stays in a hotel room which I replicated from a real hotel room in the 'New York' hotel in Rotterdam. Peter Locke checks his voicemails in his hotel room. He gets several messages from his boss saying that there's more work to do and that if he doesn't get it all done on time, he's fired. But, he also gets voicemails from his kids telling him that they love him and miss him. Peter is stressed and misses his family which really matters to him. He is a capitalistic slave. Now Peter enters a stress-induced hallucination, in which he grabs the phone and smashes it on the ground. The ground shatters and he falls through, entering 'the backrooms'. The backrooms are a 90's office like environment with pale flickering white lighting, designed to replicate the feeling of dull paper pusher. When dropped into the backrooms, Peter sees his boss walking towards him menacingly, which ofcourse is his source of anxiety and stress. Peter runs away from him trying to evade him, but he keeps on chasing Peter. While navigating through the backrooms, Peter notices posters on the walls, They are his kids drawings! Peter quickly collects every poster he sees, getting more hopeful with every single one he collects. But these posters also have a slight twist to them. They portray Peter as absent or bleeding. The latter, portraying how the kids see Peter, not physically bleeding, but hurting and suffering in his every day life due to stress and overworking.

This project was mde for a university project. My group and I got 3 aspects we had to incorporate in our project.
1) Location: "Holland-Amerika plein"
2) Explorer genre
2) VR type of game
For the location "Holland-Amerika plein" we chose the hotel called 'Hotel New York'. This is also the reason why we called it 'American Dream'.

Inside the hotel, I had replicated a hotel room and the view from that room.

For the 'explore' aspect we made the collectable posters, in this example you can see the poster and the underlying tone it has. Peter's child sees Peter hanging from a money-tree, surrounding by the words 'work, work.'

This posters is mde by my teammate Nathaniël

After a lot of research, we managed to work together live, on the same part of the project.

We presented the game at our university.

There's much more that's gone into this little project, and countless of hours of bug testing and experimentation. Working in VR was very challenging, because I had first made the project for normal PC controls, and then had to port it to VR which was quite a hassle and messed up a lot of things.
